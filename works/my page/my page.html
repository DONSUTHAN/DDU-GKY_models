Solid-state batteries are an advanced type of battery that replace the liquid electrolyte used in conventional lithium-ion batteries with a solid electrolyte. This change improves safety by reducing the risk of leakage, overheating, and fire. Solid-state batteries can offer higher energy density, meaning longer battery life in a smaller size, and they also support faster charging and longer lifespan. Although they are still expensive and difficult to mass-produce, they are considered a key future technology for smartphones, electric vehicles, and wearable devices.Silica or silicon-carbon batteries are an improved version of traditional lithium-ion batteries. They use silicon mixed with carbon in the anode instead of pure graphite. Silicon can store much more lithium, allowing the battery to achieve higher capacity without increasing size. These batteries provide better energy density, improved charging performance, and thinner designs, making them popular in modern smartphones.
